# 8 Rules of Love — Data Sampler Summary

## Core Insight

> Love is not something you feel — it's something you create, daily, through small acts of intention before the feeling arrives.

---

## Representative Samples: 3

### Sample 1: The Two-Mug Morning

**Scenario:** Sarah and Marcus fought every morning. He needed silence before human interaction; she needed connection. One day, Sarah made his coffee first — set it on his nightstand while he slept. When he emerged, she was reading peacefully. They shared a comfortable silence. He reached over and squeezed her hand.

**Pattern:** *Love is the act done before the feeling arrives.*

---

### Sample 2: The Question Behind the Question

**Scenario:** David and Lisa fought about dishes, money, soccer practice. During couples therapy, the counselor asked Lisa: "What were you *actually* afraid of?" She paused. "I'm scared that once the kids are gone, we'll have nothing left." David stared. "I had no idea you felt that way."

**Pattern:** *Behind every complaint is a fear. Behind every fear is a need.*

---

### Sample 3: The Date with Yourself

**Scenario:** Michael, 34, called himself "relationship-ready." He went to therapy, worked out, had a good job. But he lost interest in every relationship after a few weeks. His therapist asked: "When was the last time you spent an afternoon alone — not watching TV, not scrolling — just with yourself?" Michael couldn't remember.

**Pattern:** *You cannot give someone else a home you haven't built for yourself.*

---

## Key Generalizations

| Concept | Core Truth |
|---------|-----------|
| **Love is a verb** | Feelings fluctuate; actions persist |
| **Listen to the fear** | Beneath anger is always vulnerability |
| **Whole, not half** | Your relationship is only as strong as your relationship with yourself |

---

## Conclusion

Jay Shetty's *8 Rules of Love* offers a simple truth: love is a practice, not a feeling. Show up before inspiration arrives. Ask the question behind the argument. Build a home inside yourself first. Three patterns. Infinite application.
